Immediate Evacuation (generic)
Headline An immediate evacuation of [AREA] is required due to [REASON]
Instruction Everyone in [AREA] should quickly, yet safely, go to [LOCATION] by [CAR/FOOT] via [ROUTE] to [EVACUATION_POINT]. Do not block access roads or tracks. Do not take personal belongings other than critical medication and personal documents. Provide pets and other livestock with food and water. Stay out of the evacuation zone until it has been announced it is safe for you to return. Persons in areas not listed for evacuation should remain in place, and stay alert for changing conditions. Listen to the media for further instruction from local authorities.
Shelter In Place (smoke or gas)
Headline Shelter in place is declared at [LOCATION]
Description Smoke or Gas released in the area may cause negative health issues.
Instruction Go inside / Stay inside. Close all windows and secure doors. Turn off all ventilation systems such as air conditioning. Turn off open flames. If there are gaps in windows or doors, seal with tape or damp towels. Stay off the phone and listen to local media for further information from local authorities.

Continue listening to local media as updates will be provided if conditions change significantly. If you require additional information please contact [AGENCY] at [TELEPHONE NUMBER].

High wind warning
Headline High wind warning for [AREA]
Description High winds capable of causing power outages and property damage are expected.
Instruction These winds can cause lightweight objects to become dangerous airborne projectiles. High profile vehicles and vehicles pulling trailers can be flipped by crosswinds. Blowing dust can quickly reduce visibility to near zero, resulting in hazardous driving conditions and accidents involving motorists taken by surprise. Blowing dust or sand can also be a health hazard for those with respiratory problems. Secure lightweight objects. avoid traveling on roads with crosswinds.
Tsunami (Local) Evacuation Warning
Headline URGENT--TSUNAMI EVACUATION Warning for [LOCATION]. Head for higher ground immediately.
Description A tsunami has been generated and is expected to impact in [NUMBER] minutes.
Instruction All residents in [WARNING_AREA] should move immediately to high ground and away from all shorelines and low lying areas. Help persons that need assistance to evacuate. If you see unusual wave action...or water level rising or receding you may have only minutes to evacuate. Do so now. Go to your closest tsunami safety point, follow evacuation signs or ask locals. Do not take anything with you and do not block roads or paths. Stay at the tsunami safe point as the tsunami may take several hours to pass. Local authorities will tell you when it is safe.
Tsunami (Regional - Tele) Warning
Headline Regional TSUNAMI Warning for [LOCATION]. Head for higher ground immediately.
Description A Tsunami is verified to have occurred in the [LOCATION A] region that is expected to impact the Caribbean and specifically [LOCATION B] in the next [NUMBER] hours.
Instruction Prepare to head to higher ground and carefully make your way to your location tsunami evacuation point. For [LOCATION] go to [LOCATIONS]. Help persons that need assistance to evacuate. If you see unusual wave action or water level rising or receding you may have only minutes to evacuate. Do not wait until too late as tsunami can be unpredictable. Follow evacuation signs or ask locals. Take required medications, water and appropriate protection from the weather with you. Ensure pets and livestock are secured at higher ground if possible. Do not block roads or paths. Stay at the tsunami evacuation point as the tsunami may take several hours to arrive and then to pass. Local authorities will tell you if there are any updates and when it is safe to return home.
Tropical Storm Warning (48 hours)
Headline Tropical Storm Warning is issued for [AREA].
Description Tropical storm [NAME] is threatening to arrive in [NUMBER] of hours. High winds, dangerously high water and rough seas are expected.
Instruction Listen to the radio or television for tropical storm progress reports. Check emergency supplies. Fuel car. Bring in outdoor objects such as lawn furniture, toys, and garden tools and anchor objects that cannot be brought inside. Secure buildings by closing and boarding up windows. Remove outside antennas and satellite dishes. Turn refrigerator and freezer to coldest settings. Open only when absolutely necessary and close quickly. Store drinking water in clean jugs, bottles, and cooking utensils.
